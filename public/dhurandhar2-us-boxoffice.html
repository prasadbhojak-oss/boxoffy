Six days before release, Dhurandhar: The Revenge has done something no Hindi film has done before in North America. It has sold over $982,000 in US premiere tickets alone — shattering Animal's previous record of $686,000 — and racked up $3.65 million in total opening weekend pre-sales across North America with booking still very much open.

To put that in context: RRR's entire opening weekend in the US was $3.46 million. Pushpa 2 did $3.34 million. Both are considered all-time benchmarks for Indian cinema in America. Dhurandhar 2 has already surpassed both figures in advance sales alone, with organic demand and no fan club bulk booking involved.

The weekend it's walking into — Mar 20–22, 2026

This is where the story gets interesting. Dhurandhar 2 opens on the same US weekend as Project Hail Mary — Ryan Gosling's sci-fi blockbuster tracking for a $60–70M domestic opening — plus Ready or Not 2 ($11M projected). Both are mainstream Hollywood wide releases with 3,000–4,000 screens each.

Indian films typically run on 700–900 screens in the US, concentrated in major metros. That means Dhurandhar 2 does not compete directly for screens with Project Hail Mary — they are serving entirely different audiences. The real question is chart position: with a $4.5–5.5M estimated weekend, where does D2 land?

Why #4 and not higher? Project Hail Mary ($60–70M) and Hoppers ($22–26M Wk3) are simply too large in screen count to overtake. Ready or Not 2 has strong pre-sales and 2,500+ screens. But finishing #4 nationally on ~800 screens — ahead of Reminders of Him and Scream 7 on 3,000+ screens — would be an extraordinary per-screen average and a watershed moment for Bollywood in North America.

The per-screen average tells the real story. If Dhurandhar 2 grosses $5 million on 800 screens, that's a $6,250 PSA — comfortably ahead of Project Hail Mary ($17,500 on 4,000 screens), and more than double the weekend average of every film below it on the chart. By that metric, it is the most commercially efficient release of the weekend in North America.

Project Hail Mary even moved its India release to March 26 specifically to avoid competing with Dhurandhar 2 for screens and footfall in the country. Ryan Gosling's film yielding ground to a Ranveer Singh sequel in 2026 is a sentence that would have been unthinkable three years ago.

Canada — A Record In The Making

Dhurandhar Part 1 set the all-time record for an Indian film in Canada at $7.71 million lifetime. The sequel is already showing 40,000 tickets and C$600,000 in premiere pre-sales alone, with trade reports projecting that the first weekend could cross 100,000 tickets. A new Canada record is well within reach.

What to watch on opening weekend

The advance is historic. But the real test is whether the walk-up audience — the non-diaspora, neutral American moviegoer — shows up. Indian films have historically underperformed their advance in the US because the diaspora front-loads bookings in week one, and general audiences rarely follow. If D2 can hold 40–50% in week two, the North America run could push toward $12–15 million total — territory only RRR and Pushpa 2 have reached for Indian films.

The advance already guarantees a record premiere and a record opening weekend. What happens after March 22 will tell us whether Dhurandhar 2 is building a new kind of audience for Bollywood in America, or simply deepening the existing one.
