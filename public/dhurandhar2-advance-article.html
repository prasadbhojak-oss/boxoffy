When advance booking opened on March 7, the trade gave Dhurandhar 2 a two-part challenge: first break Stree 2's ₹8.75 crore Bollywood premiere record, then hunt Pawan Kalyan's OG — the ₹25 crore all-India premiere record, the highest any Indian film has ever earned from paid premieres alone. Both records now belong to Dhurandhar: The Revenge. By March 13 at 12 PM, the premiere gross stands at ₹24.76 crore (without blocked seats) and ₹29.69 crore with blocks — across 4.84 lakh tickets in 9,128 shows. The OG record is gone. The worldwide advance is approaching ₹60 crore before regular show bookings have even opened in India.

The pace has been extraordinary. From ₹19.01 crore (no blocks) on March 11 at 2 PM to ₹24.76 crore by March 13 noon — adding nearly 1.3 lakh tickets and ₹5.75 crore in gross in under 48 hours. The Hindi 2D version remains the primary engine at an ATP of ₹414, but South pricing has opened a volume play — the cheapest ticket is ₹59 at AGS Cinemas in Chennai, while Mumbai's INOX Megaplex Recliner Prime hits ₹2,900. Trade analyst Taran Adarsh confirmed the film was clocking 7,500 to 10,000 tickets per hour on BookMyShow when premiere bookings first went live — a pace rarely seen for Indian films.

“Films like Dhurandhar: The Revenge are made for the scale, sound and emotion that only cinemas can deliver. We have already surpassed 150,000 tickets in advance sales at PVR INOX alone.”
Gautam Dutta, CEO PVR INOX — to Variety India, March 11, 2026

The national chains are at capacity. PVR INOX CEO Gautam Dutta called the response a "big screen celebration" and confirmed 1.5 lakh+ tickets in PVR INOX chains alone. Wednesday March 18 PLF shows across the US and Canada have sold out at multiple locations (Variety). North America advance for the opening weekend is nearing $2 million across 678 locations — on track to surpass several pan-India films' overseas OW totals (Sacnilk). In India, 5,00,000 BMS users have marked interest for the sequel — Dhurandhar 1 sold 1.3 crore tickets on BMS, the highest for any Hindi film on the platform.

The overseas picture is historic for a Hindi film. The US premiere pre-sales alone crossed $982,374 across 678 locations by March 13, surpassing Animal's all-time Bollywood US premiere record. North America opening weekend advance is approaching $2M. The worldwide advance total — India + overseas — is around ₹60 crore gross, and this is before regular admission bookings have opened in India. When they do, the ₹100 crore combined premiere+Day 1 advance target becomes realistic.

New: Yami Gautam confirmed in cast. Runtime: 3 hrs 55 mins. Regular India show bookings not yet open — all figures above are premiere-only advance.

Boxoffy Verdict — Mar 11, 2026

The record books are being rewritten in real time. Dhurandhar 2 is not merely tracking well — it has already become the biggest paid preview event in Bollywood history, needing just ₹1.99 crore more to claim the all-India record from OG. With 8 days still to release, the full advance booking for the opening weekend not yet open, and South India showing unprecedented fervour for a Hindi film, March 18's premiere night is going to be something Indian cinema has never quite seen before. The reckoning arrives at 5 PM on March 18.
