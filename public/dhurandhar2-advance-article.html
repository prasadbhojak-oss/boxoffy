When advance booking opened on March 7, the trade gave Dhurandhar 2 a target: break Stree 2's ₹8.75 crore net paid preview record for a Hindi film. It did that in under 24 hours. The question that followed was whether it could challenge OG — Pawan Kalyan's ₹25 crore all-India premiere record, the highest ever for any Indian film. As of March 11 at 2 PM, with the premiere gross at ₹19.01 crore and 3.56 lakh tickets across 8,371 shows, the OG record is within a single day's momentum.

The surge in the last 24 hours has been staggering. The film stood at ₹15.91 crore (no blocks) on Tuesday March 10 at 2 PM. By Wednesday March 11 at 2 PM, that figure had jumped to ₹19.01 crore — nearly a 20% single-day increase, adding roughly 70,000 tickets in one day. When blocked seats are included, the total stands at ₹24.2 crore, with the Hindi 2D version contributing ₹18.51 crore alone at an average ticket price of ₹414.

“Films like Dhurandhar: The Revenge are made for the scale, sound and emotion that only cinemas can deliver. We have already surpassed 150,000 tickets in advance sales at PVR INOX alone.”
Gautam Dutta, CEO PVR INOX — to Variety India, March 11, 2026

The national chains tell the story precisely. As of 11 PM on March 11, Pinkvilla reports 2.20 lakh tickets sold in PVR INOX and Cinepolis alone for the Wednesday March 18 paid previews — with an additional 12,000 admissions from dubbed versions. The momentum is described as holding steady at 26,000 new tickets per day in national chains, which is extraordinary for the middle portion of a pre-sales window.

The overseas picture is equally historic. US opening day advance has crossed $1.12 million, with the full opening weekend at $2.16 million across 557 locations — beats Jawan's US premiere pre-sales and is closing in on Animal's all-time Bollywood US premiere record of $1.2 million for Day 0 alone. The film hasn't even opened its general admission booking for the weekend yet.

Boxoffy Verdict — Mar 11, 2026

The record books are being rewritten in real time. Dhurandhar 2 is not merely tracking well — it has already become the biggest paid preview event in Bollywood history, needing just ₹1.99 crore more to claim the all-India record from OG. With 8 days still to release, the full advance booking for the opening weekend not yet open, and South India showing unprecedented fervour for a Hindi film, March 18's premiere night is going to be something Indian cinema has never quite seen before. The reckoning arrives at 5 PM on March 18.
